IDEA 2 | Identifying the Problem Space

The deadline for this assignment is set to September 23 to nudge everyone into starting the discussion. The deadline to submit your finished work is 11:59 PM, October 5.

In this discussion-style assignment, you will contribute to your classmate's project. 

You have made a clear statement on what you want to do for your project in your Project Assignment. The project that you proposed is likely based on your experience. Still, you need to ensure that your experience is not an isolated incident but a shared experience. To do so, we are conducting an open-ended interview with users. 

 Overview

  • Two students are assigned to this group discussion.
  • You will interview each other focusing on how your partner thinks about your idea as users.
  • You will take turn and interview each other.
  • The focus of the interview is to check if there is a shared experience related to the idea that you intend to pursue this semester.
  • I recommend starting the conversation by briefly explaining your project in a couple of sentences.
  • To make sure nobody is wrongfully penalized for their partner's participation, or lack thereof, all communication for this assignment should take place either on this discussion board or in your recorded meeting sessions. Grades for this assignment will be based on the information on this discussion board and your meeting record (likely Microsoft Teams) only. No interviewer will be penalized due to inadequate participation from the interviewee. The interviewee will receive the penalty instead.

 Interviewees (i.e., Users)

You do not have to prepare much for taking the role as an interviewee. It would be nice if you can refer to the concepts that you learned in the course when answering the questions but that is not a requirement. It is also nice to have your camera on if the meeting is conducted virtually. I recommend participating without knowing what the application is. You can ask what the app is at the end or after the interview.

The most important job as an interviewer is to be respectful for your classmates time. Make sure that you prioritize helping your classmate's assignment first as an interviewee, especially if you are busy or have limited availability. You will lose all points for this assignment and the portion for this assignment in the final paper if you create excessive hinderance to your classmate's learning experience.

 Interviewers

Before the interview 

  • Closely read the Chapter excerpt for this assignment. 
  • The topic of the interview is people's experience with existing apps, or, functions or services within apps, that are comparable to your project. 
  • Note you are NOT asking questions about your project specifically. 
  • Conduct 10-minute (approximately) interview with your partner about an existing app that is comparable to your project. 
    • Your interviewees (aka users) should be fairly knowledgeable of the application/device/service to the extent that you do not have to explain the app's function. 
    • The interview must be either a structured or semi-structured interview, meaning the core questions must be prepared before the meeting. 
  • Prepare the interview questions.
    • Your interviewees' answers can be very short, no matter how hard you try. Expect a 30-second long answer per question, but make sure to have some spare questions under your sleeve. 
    • Some example questions:
      • What is your experience with [Comparable Apps] in general?
        • What are your overall feelings when you use on the  [Comparable Apps]?
        • What are you expecting to achieve with the  [Comparable Apps]?
        • How would you go about [specific task] using this product?
        • How often do you use them?
        • Are there any alternatives that you know?
      • What is something that you like about the  [Comparable Apps]?
      • What is something that you hate about the  [Comparable Apps]? 
        • What do you want to change about the  [Comparable Apps]?
        • Can you think of any alternatives?
        • Do you have any other suggestions or feature request?
    • Why do you use [Comparable Apps] for another [Comparable Apps]?
    • Would you recommend certain [Comparable Apps] to others? Why or why not?

During the interview 

  • Record the whole interview. 
  • At the beginning of the interview, state the purpose of your interview. Ask if they agree to record the interview and share the recording with others (the instructor in the course). Also, state that their personal identifiers will be removed in any audio or text file that you create out of the interview, although private information is not in the interest of the interview. 
  • I recommend having the interview on Teams, even if you plan to conduct the interview in person. Teams can record and transcribe the interview for you. 
  • Go through the interview questions that you prepared. Do not stop your interviewee as much as possible. It usually is a good sign if the interviewee's answer is longer. 

After the interview 

  • Summary of each Interview: Reduce the answer for each question to one or two sentences. 
  • After you go through all the interviews, go through the answers that you received and find Themes. 
  • Themes: The themes you find here are the problem spaces that you claim to have discovered. Simply put, themes are similarities that you find across the interviews. They can be considered as clues for your investigation. This can be a common need, dissatisfaction, requirement, or preference that the interviewers have in common. Not all your interviewees have to carry or agree with the theme. 
  • Number of themes: at least one; while there is no limit to the number of themes that you find in an interview, I recommend keeping it less than three for this assignment. 
  • Keep an open mind when identifying themes. One of the best possible outcomes of this assignment is finding a brand new idea for an app that can alter your creative direction.

Submit your assignment 

Submit the recordings: The recording should be reasonably audible and should run through the whole interview to count. Audio is required; video is optional. Two of you may submit a single interview recording if it includes both interviews.

Submit your analysis write-up: 

  1. Each interviewer submits their write-up.
  2. Attach and post a DOCX or a PDF file in this discussion. 
  3. The DOCX/PDF file should include:
    • A Summary of the Interview.
    • Report the core questions and their answers.
    • Describe the themes that you found. More than one theme should be reported.
    • What are the themes or ideas in the responses from the interview that you were similar to yours? Explain why that matters. If there is none, also explain and recognize why that matters.
    • Conclude the report by stating how you will incorporate the findings from the interviews into your project. This application/incorporation part should be as specific as possible.
  4. 1000 - 1500 words (2-3 pages)
