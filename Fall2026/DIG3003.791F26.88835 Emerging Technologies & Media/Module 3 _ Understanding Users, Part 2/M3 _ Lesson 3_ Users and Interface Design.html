Users and Interaction Design

Communication technology has revolutionized the way we connect and interact with each other, and its impact on the world cannot be overstated. The advancements in communication technology have brought numerous benefits, transforming various aspects of our lives, including business, education, healthcare, and social interactions. One of the key aspects of modern communication technology is its ability to bridge distances and enable instant global connectivity. The advent of the internet, smartphones, and social media platforms has made it possible to communicate with people from all corners of the world in real time. This has opened up new opportunities for collaboration, commerce, and cultural exchange on a global scale. End users, more simply users, or individuals who use communication technology for personal or professional purposes, play a crucial role in driving and shaping this transformation. They are the ones who adopt and utilize these technologies, pushing for further innovation and improvement. End users have the power to influence the development and direction of communication technology by demanding user-friendly interfaces, enhanced features, and more secure platforms.

Furthermore, end users are the beneficiaries of the positive impact of communication technology. It has empowered individuals to have a voice, express their opinions, and share their experiences with a global audience. Social media platforms have become powerful tools for activism, enabling people to raise awareness, organize movements, and drive social change. Individuals can now easily access information, learn new skills, and connect with like-minded individuals, regardless of geographical boundaries. This all means that it is integral to better understand users to better understand how technology affects the way we communicate. As Journalism and Digital Communication majors, you want to better understand how users experience the mediated experiences with the technology so that you can create a more potent and meaningful message for others. In other words, we want to understand the user experience during mediated communication.
