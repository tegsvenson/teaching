Media Diary 2_ Post Your Daily Journal

See Module 0 or start from this page if you are not sure what this assignment is about.

In Media Diary 2, you will make entries to your diary. This page is technically a group discussion board with a group member of 1, which means only you and the instructor can read or write here. We are utilizing the discussion function to keep track of the time and dates of your diary entries.

Data Collection & Daily Entry Period

The data collection and daily journal entry period starts on Tuesday, September 2,  and concludes on Friday, September 12. The deadline is set to September 2 in the system to prompt you to start working on this assignment. 

Within those dates, make a total of five diary entries and analyze them for full credit. Put the date of the day that you are analyzing. Since we are using a discussion board, the records will show when you posted your diary. Each diary should be about the day before to receive full credit.

Data with Purpose

You will collect data about yourself and analyze the data for a week. First, think about why and how this data collection would result in a meaningful outcome. What do you think your current habits are, off the top of your head? What are some of the potential benefits of working on a media diary? The following website offers some food for thought: quantifiedself.com

Once you have established your goals, have a clear plan for what data you are going to collect and exactly how you’re going to collect it. 

Kinds of data

You will be collecting quantitative data on how much time you spend using various apps such as Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, WhatsApp, or any other apps as well as how much you pick up your phone.

Screen Time (iOS) or Digital Wellbeing (Android) will generate both numbers and charts of these usages. You can’t export the raw data from your phone. At the end of the assigned week you must take and post screenshots of the following:

  • The chart showing the amount of time you spent using each app each day during the week
  • The chart showing your total screen time for the week
  • The chart showing the number of times per day you picked up (or unlocked) your phone during the week
  • Data showing the how often and how much you used different apps on your phone 
  • Connect the data with what happened outside of your interaction with your phone on the day.

Reflect on the data

Within each entry, after summarizing the observed behavior (i.e., time spent on device), interpret that day's behavior briefly. As part of this assignment, throughout the period you must keep a daily journal in which you step outside yourself to observe your use of media technology. For full credit, you should have at least 200 word per entry. Ideally, the journal entry should happen no later than the next day. 

You can make this as fun, funny or serious as you want, just so long as what you write is accurate. The journal must be written in third person, by referring to yourself as "he," "she," ''they," or other, or by name. The observer is watching you throughout the day, describing your behavior with your mobile phone, for example.  Here's a sample entry:

Nearly every time he stopped at a red light, he pulled out the device and started manipulating it, dragging his finger down the screen, pausing now and then to scan what he saw. Often it was news reports from CNN or else something called Facebook where he connected with his "friends," most often Martha and Josey. They didn't "talk" about much, just posted funny stuff like jokes or memes. One of them showed [post an example]. 

It's essential that you keep a journal because you will be using sections of it to flesh our your Media Diary | Concluding Report. With a diary to draw from, you will have a difficult time reaching the 1,000 words required for the report.
