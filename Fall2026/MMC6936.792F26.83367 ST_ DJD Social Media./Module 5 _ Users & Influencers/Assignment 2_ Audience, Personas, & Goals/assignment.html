3. Define the "Key Points of Target Audience"

We have four criteria to define based on research on IBIS or other reputable sources: age, gender, stage of life, and wants/needs.

Try to be as specific as possible. At the very least, your source should directly point to a specific part of the IBIS summary that you found.

At least 2 out of 4 elements should be based on your findings from IBIS World.

Avoid targeting everyone or nearly everyone.

  • This applies even if you intend your brand to be inclusive. A brand that supports everyone and a brand that's supported by everyone are two different concepts. See here for more   Considerations and opportunities for brand managers | Journal Article.
    Ferraro, Carla, et al. "Embracing Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion (DEI): Considerations and Opportunities for Brand Managers,." Business Horizons, vol. 66, no. 4, 2023, pp. 463-479, https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0007681322001240

 

4. Create an Audience Persona

After you've done research on your target audience and filled in the template, create a persona to represent your target audience. Your persona should fit within the audience segmentation details you've just defined and act as a personification of your target audience. The idea of a persona is that you can refer to this "person" when defining your social media strategies and goals.
