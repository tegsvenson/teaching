Discussion 2_ Brand Proposal

Module 2 | Brand Proposal: Create your brand

  Discussion Board Instructions 

1. Your Brand:

Create a hypothetical brand. Your brand should be something specific. Here are several examples and ideas:

  •  Products: It can be any kind of product on social media
    • Still, make sure it is something that is fit for promotion on social media.
    • For example, you can create a clothing line, cosmetics brand, or beverage brand.
    • Marketing a product will involve creating at least one image of a prototype.
    • Be as specific as possible when you describe your brand.
    • Your brand should not be a parody of an existing brand to provide room for the marketer to create a unique brand image.
  • Services: You can also create a service.
    • You can sell a service,
    • For example, you can create a brand about customizing and selling sneakers.
    • Another example: you can have a wine delivery/subscription service as your brand.
    • You can also create a news channel that covers news/reviews/magazines.
      • Creating this type of brand is generally not recommended. Why? Since this can be redundant to other assignments that you have done or will do in other content-creation-focused classes.
      • I will comment and categorize your brand as a news channel, where applicable.
      • If the brand is a news channel, the marketer is required to have a creation/curation ratio of 8:2, compared to the regular 5:5.

2. Pick A Brand Name:

Once you've narrowed your brand, pick a name. This will be the name that you use for branding purposes across social media. 

3. Turn It In

That's it. Once the brand name is picked, post the brand's name, and describe it using more than three sentences. The deadline for posting your brand idea is 11:59 pm, September 7. I will comment on your post in case there are any issues. These issues should be addressed by 11:59 pm, September 9. 

 

Note: You will not work on the brand that you propose here for the main project assignment in this course, the Social Media Marketing Project. For more details, see the instructions in our next discussion. Note that the next discussion will decide the nature of your project and that it is on a first-come-first-serve base.
