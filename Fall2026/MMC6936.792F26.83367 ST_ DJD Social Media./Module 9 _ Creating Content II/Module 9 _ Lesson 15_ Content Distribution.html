3. Can I use AI to generate images or text?

AI is still pretty dumb, especially with complicated and multifaceted tasks. I do neither prohibit nor encourage use of generative artificial intelligence or algorithms for completing assignments. By default, I do not check if you used AI or not in your assignment.

Problems usually occur when students entrust AI too much and let them make important decisions. It is very challenging to correctly fathom when that "too much" happens, especially for a topic that you are currently learning. Trust me; my main research area has been human-computer interaction with virtual agents (which is what we call AI these days) for the past decade, even before the current AI boom.

For example, the image that you need to create have many rules. You have set up color schemes, specific fonts, and more that constitutes the brand's overall tone. If you think you can create images that fits the bill, and if those images are consistent through different posts, sure, why not. You may end up spending more time (and potentially money) trying to come up with images that satisfy all the requirements. 

In short, you may use AI, at your own risk.
