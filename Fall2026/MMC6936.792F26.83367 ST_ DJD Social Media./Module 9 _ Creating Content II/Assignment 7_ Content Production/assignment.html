Module 9 | Assignment 7: Content Production

Note: Work on Assignment 6: Editorial Calendar together with this one. This is so you know exactly what pieces of content you will be responsible for.

  Assignment Instructions 

1. Keep your schedule and content synced.

In tandem with the Editorial Calendar assignment, materialize the 11 (or more) pieces of content that you will be responsible for. The following are the requirements: 

  • At least 4 of your posts must be original, created content from scratch (employing stock photos/videos is acceptable as original post). The remaining can be either created or curated (shared from somewhere/someone else), depending on your plans.
  • One of your created post must be a scheduled Livestream event. The livestream event must consist of two parts:
    1. A simple advertisement for your livestream event. You can reuse your brand's logo or template for this.
    2. The livestream event.

If you decide to create the minimum number of created content, the following will be the composition of different posts in your campaign:

Total Number of Posts (11) = Curated Content (5) + Created Content (4) + Livestream Ad and Event (2)

 

2. Create Your Content

The following are the requirements for the four or more pieces of created content that you will create:

  • Identifiers (e.g., post numbers or titles) for each created post, synced with your Editorial Calendar.
  • At least two pieces of content that are be finished, ready to be posted. This includes videos, images, sounds, texts, and captions for your posts.
  • Specific action plan on the content that is WIP:
    • Graphics you plan on creating should be finished now;
    • Content created with stock images or video should be finished now;
    • Content created with your own photos/video should be planned out now. If you have not taken the photos or video yet, that is okay, but I want you to turn in your work in progress-- any templates you have started, any graphics you plan on adding, any video outlines you have, steps you will take to complete that content, and anything else you think necessary.
  • Reference your content for any parts of it that was imported, provide the following as appendix:
    • Links to original images if you sourced images from repositories such as Adobe Stock
    • The original image if you took them
    • AI prompts and responses if you used generative AI

This means the following is the minimum requirement:

Total Number of Created Posts (4) = Finished Content Pieces (2) + Content 

 

3. Curated Content

The following are the requirements for the content that you will curate:

  • A description of specific posts that you will use
  • Links to at least one post that you have decided to curate

 

3. Plan Your Livestream

For your Livestream, you should:

First, create a post to advertise you'll be hosting a Livestream on X date and time. This post should be planned for the same platform that the Livestream will be streamed on; include the date and time of the Livestream; and include the topic(s) the Livestream will cover. Feel free to include additional information as you see fit (i.e., who is hosting, any other Livestreams coming soon from your group, etc.)

Then, create an outline of your Livestream. I want you to write out step-by-step instructions for yourself on what your Livestream will include. You should plan for: a clear beginning with an introduction of yourself and what you will cover; your main content; and a clear closing and/or opportunity to ask questions. If you are aiming to keep your Livestream within a certain time, you can add timestamp goals for yourself. 

 

4. Turn it In

Submit a single PDF file that includes:

  1. All of your created pieces of content that are finished,
  2. Any created content that are works-in-progress, with comments on how you plan on completing them,
  3. A Description and links to the posts that you will curate,
  4. Your Livestream advertisement post graphic, and 
  5. Your Livestream outline.

Deadline: You have until 11:59 pm, October 26.
