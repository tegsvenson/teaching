Your Editorial Calendar

As you've seen from this module's readings, an editorial calendar is essential for organizing your content creation and social media strategy. Let's try creating one ourselves.

  Assignment Instructions 

1. Consider the best posting strategy.

Based on this module's readings and the information on the best times/days/frequencies to post across various social platforms, you should get a rough idea of the best way to distribute your content. You are required to provide a rationale on your created posts in your final paper.

Below are the requirements for your posts:

    • You must have 10 posts (11 if you include the Livestream Ad):
    • At least  5 posts must be created. The rest you can plan to be created or curated, depending on your brand's chosen ratio. (Example: if your brand's created/curated ratio is 8/2, then you can plan to make 8 posts and curate 2). 
    • One of your created posts should be a live stream. 
    • The posting period should fall within the following dates: November 6 - November 26
    • These posts can be split across different platforms (i.e., 4 Facebook posts, 4 Instagram, 2 TikTok, etc.) if you have more than one platform in your plan.
    • You can post the same content on your different platforms. They will count as a single post unless they are meaningfully distinct.

2. Make an account on Trello.

There is plenty of software out there that specializes in creating editorial calendars for social strategists. However, the majority of them are pay-to-use. For this class, we'll be using a compatible software called Trello. 

Let's go to Trello.com and sign up/in. 

3. Create a board.

 Once you are signed in on Trello, create a board:  

  • To do this, you'll need to create a "Board." You can give the Board any theme, style, colors, background, etc., that you like.
  • Give it a title, something like "My Social Media Editorial Calendar."
  • These are actual posting dates; make sure the dates are well-reasoned and feasible. 

4. Schedule your Brand Posts.

Add a "card" to the board to represent a post you will publish for your brand. We will have one board and 10 cards.

Below are the requirements for each card on the board. Each card should:

    • Represent a specific post. You can do this by giving each post a number or a (working) title. The title/number you use here should match those in your Assignment 7.
    • Specify the date and time of the day that you'll be posting
    • Specify what platform you will post to
    • Specify the type of content you are creating (i.e., video, tutorial, graphic, article, etc.).
