Course Overview

Course Overview

In this survey course, students learn the impact of technology in communication with an emphasis on the psychological aspects at an individual user level, which extends to a discussion about how this can influence the media industry and society. Psychological, ethical, and legal concepts that are related to technologies and communication are covered.

Course objectives

In this course, we will cover the following objectives:

  • Understand how new technology emerges and affects individuals and society.
  • Understand how the advancement in technology can be shaped by other aspects of a society.
  • Understand how technology affects the way people communicate with each other through media.
  • Learn key psychological theories that explain how and why people perceive, use, accept/reject technology.
  • Study key emerging technologies that are related to communication (e.g., virtual/augmented/mixed reality, virtual agents, and artificial intelligence).
  • Understand the ethical and legal implications of communication technologies.
  • Learn the essential considerations and concepts that are related to interface design and how they affect users.
  • Apply knowledge in communication technology and user experience to problem-solving.
  • Apply theoretical and practical principles to strategically design a mediated experience for existing or novel applications.

Student Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, you will be able to:

  1. Identify problems and solutions in regard to technology in communication.
  2. Learn about the major drivers of technological advancement and how it affects the way people communicate.
  3. Understand the user/receiver perspectives of communication to communicate with others more effectively.
  4. Learn the core psychological underpinnings and its concepts related to user experience in emerging media technologies.
  5. Identify key emerging technologies that are related to communication (e.g., virtual/augmented/mixed reality, virtual agents, and artificial intelligence).
  6. Understand the ethical and legal implications of communication technologies.
  7. Understand the practical concepts that are related to user experience.
  8. Critique existing communication or media interface/application/services from a user perspective.
  9. Learn the method and tools for conducting a qualitative approach to gathering and analyzing data about user experiences.
  10. Learn how to use prototyping tools to apply and test ideas to an application.
  11. Apply theoretical and practical principles to strategically design a mediated experience for existing or novel applications.

 Academic Integrity

Academic integrity is a cornerstone of any educational institution, and it is especially crucial in an online business course where the opportunities for collaboration and information sharing are vast. As a student in this course, you are expected to uphold the highest standards of honesty and personal integrity in your academic work.

It is important to note that all assignments should be completed independently unless explicitly stated otherwise. While you are permitted to use AI tools to assist you in your assignments, it is essential to understand that the true value of your education comes from your own critical thinking and effort. Relying solely on AI may not provide the deep understanding and skills that you will need in the business world.

When using any external sources, including AI, citations must be provided where required. This not only gives credit to the original creators but also reinforces the transparency and honesty that are expected in all your academic endeavors.

Quizzes and exams has an open-book policy. This means that you may refer to established documentations (materials from this course) to answer the questions. Information from this course's reading material is prioritized as the basis of your grade.

By adhering to these principles, you demonstrate your commitment to academic integrity and the value you place on a genuine and meaningful education. Let's work together to maintain a culture of honesty and respect in our academic community.
