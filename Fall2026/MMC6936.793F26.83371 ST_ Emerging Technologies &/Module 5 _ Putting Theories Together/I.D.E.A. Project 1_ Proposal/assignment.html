IDEA 1 | Proposal

Throughout this semester, you create a prototype for an interactive media tool. For example:

  • A completely new application or a website*
  • An existing app but with added functionality**
  • An existing app, existing functionality, but tweaked to significantly improve user experience

You will work on different aspects of the project, see this page for an overview of the series of assignments.

The required reading for this module nicely covers the user-centric development cycle, so go ahead and take a look at it before you start this assignment. If you use external sources for your arguments, they will only receive points if you provide the references and citations properly.

The proposal should not be longer than 2500 words. The amount of text is not a grading criterion; all requirements for this assignments would likely be covered in about 1500 words. You will likely be asked to change/add/remove ideas from your proposal and resubmit them after the first meeting.

Ideation

Describe what is needed and why it’s needed. The interactive media that you will create can be a brand-new app or service, a new function of an existing app, or a significant and demonstrable improvement in terms of user experience for an existing app or its specific function. Focus on the user’s needs – your ideas do not have to be groundbreaking. However, if there is zero user need for your idea, or there is no problem space, to begin with, the idea will not survive the process when we later test the ideas using qualitative and quantitative methods.

Take a close look at the rubrics to better understand the requirements for this proposal. You will find that you are asked to apply the fundamental concepts that we covered in Modules 1, 2, and 3

Feasibility

Explain how you will be able to materialize your idea within this semester. See future assignments to have a better idea of what you will do. You will have to create a working, interactive prototype using one of the accessible tools suggested in this course. Therefore, make sure that this is something you can materialize. A brand new idea for a video game on Vision Pro sounds definitely interesting, but it will be extremely challenging to create a prototype within just a semester.

Requirements

Throughout the series of assignments, the change, improvement, revision, or novelty in user experience should satisfy both of the following:

  1. Something that you designed and materialized. The core usability improvement should not be outsourced. For example, "I will create a completely new content recommendation algorithm" is not a good idea for the purpose of this course, unless you are creating the algorithm itself (hint: leveraging on AI implementation will likely not work). 
  2. Your argument for claiming why this will work, should be based on firm theoretical backgrounds. In the rubrics you will find Usability Goal, Design Principle, Information Processing, and Media Effect. These all consist of a very unique set of ideas that are covered in this course's lessons and readings. Having external sources with proper citations as your rationale is welcomed but scrutinized.

Feels like a lot? It will not be easy. That is why you will have another chance to revise the proposal for a full grade after having an individual meeting with me.
