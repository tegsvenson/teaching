Module 3 | Lesson 3: Marketing Team Composition

This written lesson demonstrates what kind of jobs there are in a social media marketing team, and how each of those positions is related to the assignments in this course. Breckenridge in Social Media and Public Relations identifies 8 roles that a modern communicator should play in terms of social media management.

The first 5 out of 8 Roles in a Social Media Marketing Team

Sometimes you work as a one-person team (i.e., full-stack marketer), or sometimes you work as a team with others. This depends on various factors, some of them being the size of the company, project, campaign, or your proficiencies on the tasks involved in these roles. In this class, you will work as a one-person team and carry out your project. Among the 8 positions, we cover 5 of them in this course(click on the tabs to see description for each):

Internal Collaboration Generator

In a normal social-media marketing setting, this individual understands that social media marketing involves organizing different tasks. This person's role is to monitor, set up assignments and schedules for a campaign, and facilitate and manage team assignments and communications if you were working in a team. In terms of assignments in this class, this person's task is Social Media Audit (this is your first assignment due in module 4) and also the Finalized Marketing and Content Plan (this is your final assignment).

Relationship Analyzer

This role is an "audience specialist:" They understand how audiences connect with the brand and help create a strategy to reflect audience preferences. This role is ideal for a marketing major. In this course's terms, this person's job would be to lead the Audience, Personas, and Goals (this is your second assignment). 

Reputation Manager

Also referred to in the original as "Reputation Task Force Professional," This role focuses on the reputation management of a brand, which is essential for any company involved in social media. Their focus is on brand voice and the company profile. In this class, this role involves a good sense of design and will spearhead the Branding Profile (this is your third assignment)

Communications Organizer

This individual is a communication specialist who will monitor social media strategy as it relates to communication between your company (group) members and audience. Specifically, in this class, this role is related to Algorithm & SEO Strategies (this is your fourth assignment).

Metrics Master

This role analyzes measurable objects of social media and relates them to the goals of a brand or company. This role involves a considerable understanding regarding numbers work. The Metrics Master's task is related to the Analytics & Revenue Potential Report (this is your 10 assignment).

 

3 Roles That We Don't in This Course

There are three roles we are not worrying about in this class. These are not necessarily less important then the other 5 that we cover. Rather, they are not fit for the purpose of this course. They are (Click each dropdown to learn more).

PR Policymaker

Breckenridge describes this role as the individual who determines the social media communications strategies of a brand and gives social media training to a company's staff. For the purposes of this class, that role is me. 😊

Technology Tester

This name describes the role of a person in the social media field who stays up-to-date with the latest apps and technology. Because this class uses a certain set of social media and software, there is no need for this role.

Pre-Crisis Doctor

This is a vital role for any smart company to have, as pre-crisis management --especially in a social media setting-- helps ensure a brand is prepared to take a proactive approach to a crisis should one occur. For this class, any crises will be handled on a one-on-one basis with me.

Reference

Breckenridge, D. (2012). Social media and public relations: Eight new practices for the PR professional. Pearson Education.
