What's Already There & What Can We Do?

Now that you've learned about internal and external analyses, for this assignment, you will conduct an external social media audit for your brand (and by your brand, the brand you picked).

  Assignment Instructions 

1. Canva

First, make sure to log into Canva, and open up this social media audit template. You can create your own copy of the template using the option in the menu, "File - Make a Copy".

 

2. External Audit/Competitive Analysis

Note. I strongly recommend you review this week's lessons, especially Lesson 6, before working on this assignment. Attempting to wing it may result in large point deduction and revisions.

Pick 4 competing brands. By competing brands, I mean 4 brands that have the same general purpose, content, and audience as yours. You will analyze these brands' social presence.

Fill in the social media audit spreadsheet. This includes:

  • Write a general overview of the strengths and weaknesses you notice about each brand.
  • Identifying the 2 most prominent social platforms that each brand uses (note that most brands will use more than 2 platforms -- the core platforms are their most important ones).
  • Calculating the created-to-curated content ratio: you do this by going through the last 5 posts on each brand's core platforms, and comparing how many were created (original) posts and how many were curated (shared) posts.
  • For example, if a page's last 5 posts consisted of 3 original posts and 2 curated posts, the created: curated content ratio would be 3:2.
  • Finally, look at each brand's success metrics: out of the last 5 posts on the brand's core platform, what was the average success rate? To calculate this, add up the amount of likes/shares/views for the last 5 posts, then divide that number by 5 to calculate the average success metric per post.
  • Feel free to adjust the fonts and colors, or even formatting, of the spreadsheet to match your branding ideas.
  • If some of the metrics (e.g., likes, shares, or views) are not accessible for the brands or social media platforms, make sure to omit them across the board.

  

3. SWOT Analysis:

Based on your research on #2 External Audit, identify:

  • Strengths: What sets your brand apart?
  • Weaknesses: What can you improve?
  • Opportunities: What/who are your targets?
  • Threats: How does your system work against competition?

Each of S, W, O, and T should be directly and clearly based on findings of #2, External Audit.

Alternatively, you may cite other reliable resources by citing them as your rationale. This rationale be specific to your brand.

 

4. Brand Proposal:

Based on your SWOT analysis, create your brand proposal by answering questions on Page 5.

You may cite other reliable resources by citing them as your rationale. This rationale be specific to your brand.

More than half of the questions on this page should be properly answered for full credit in this part.

 

5. Turn It In:

Submit two versions:  (1) Submit the compiled PDF document, and (2) leave the Canva link as a comment under your submission. All versions should be submitted to receive grades.
