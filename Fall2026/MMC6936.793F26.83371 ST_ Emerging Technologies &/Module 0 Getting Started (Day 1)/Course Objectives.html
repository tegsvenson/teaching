Course Introduction

 Course Description

Social media literacy is critical in today’s society for both professional communicators and consumers of communication. This class covers what social media are, who uses them, who gains from them, and how they are transforming our personal, social, cultural, political, and economic worlds as well as our media. You will become familiar with a range of social media tools, analyze and discuss their uses and implications, and use them in a professional manner.

 

 Course Objectives

At the conclusion of this course, students are expected to be able to:

    1. Understand how and when social media emerged and evolved into its current state, focusing on journalism and communication-related professions.
    2. Study top social sites (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, YouTube, etc.), and understand their strengths and specific functions, which relate to their target audiences.
    3. Understand the ethical and legal complexities, including privacy concerns, that are posed to journalism and communication professionals while using social media.
    4. Understand users’ behavioral and psychological underpinnings for using social sites; understand how social sites change and drive news and media consumption.
    5. Understand the revenue monetization potential for social media accounts.
    6. Learn about social media branding strategies, techniques, and tools.
    7. Learn strategies and tools for growing followers, networking, and engaging with others through social media.
    8. Learn the best guidelines and good practices of content production for social media, including using SM for live streaming, content curation, multimedia production, brand promotion, and news.
    9. Learn to use social media tools and metrics to analyze account performance and improve account performance based on these analytics.
    10. Integrate design principles in the planning, organization, and creation of visual communications.

 Student Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course, you should be able to demonstrate knowledge and skills in the following areas:

  1. Understand the history, evolution, and current practices of social media in journalism and communication-related professions.
  2. Understand consumers’ behaviors, motivations, and gratifications in using social sites; understand how social sites change and drive news and media consumption.
  3. Learn about social media branding strategies, techniques, and tools.
  4. Learn strategies and tools for growing followers, networking, and engaging with consumers and peers through social media.
  5. Learn the best guidelines and good practices of content production for social media, including using SM for live streaming, content curation, multimedia production, brand promotion, and news.
  6. Learn how to uphold social media content to traditional journalistic and communication norms and ethics, such as fact-checking, verification, accuracy, objectivity, and ethics.
  7. Study top social sites (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, YouTube, etc.), and understand the strengths and specific functions of each site and the specifics of their audiences.
  8. Learn to use social media tools and metrics to analyze account performance and improve account performance based on these analytics.
  9. Understand the ethical and legal complexities, including privacy concerns, that are posed to journalism and communication professionals while using social media.
  10. Understand the revenue monetization potential for social media accounts.
